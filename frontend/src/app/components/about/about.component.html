What is the Arkham Analysis?

Not a deck builder. Not a collection tracker. The Arkham Analysis is a reading tool — a way to understand the patterns behind investigator choices, the mathematics behind the chaos bag, and the threat profile of every scenario across every campaign.

It draws on published deck data, official card text, and game rules to surface what the numbers actually say. What cards do Agnes players keep reaching for? Which investigator survives a Carcosa bag at Expert? How dangerous is this scenario compared to the last one?

These are the questions it answers. No oracles. Just patterns and probability.

Where the Data Comes From

Transparency about sources, freshness, and scope

ArkhamDB

Deck statistics — investigator popularity, card inclusion rates, XP trends, and faction meta share — are sourced from published deck records on ArkhamDB, the community's canonical deck-sharing platform. The data covers the last 90 days of published decks by default.

Card & Scenario Data

Card text, investigator stats, scenario encounter decks, and chaos bag compositions are drawn from ArkhamDB's public card API and maintained locally. Scenario context calculations (threat ratings, token modifiers, skill test distributions) are computed server-side from that card data.

Data Freshness

Dashboard meta stats are cached and refreshed every Sunday at 00:00 UTC. This means the deck trends, investigator rankings, and faction meta share you see reflect the state of the community as of the most recent Sunday — not live data. Scenario and card data updates when the local database is rebuilt.
